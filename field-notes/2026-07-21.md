# Field Notes — 2026-07-21

## Signal: fluid antennas

Started from `Spatial Limits of Fluid Antenna Systems` and wandered into FAS/FAMA, movable antennas, reconfigurable antennas, mmWave surface-wave FAS hardware, and channel acquisition problems.

The recurring pattern: wireless research is giving the antenna itself more degrees of freedom. The radio endpoint becomes a body that can search a local field, not just a fixed transducer running smarter DSP.

## Sharp observation

The most important contradiction is not whether FAS can theoretically exploit spatial diversity. It is whether the device can know enough about unobserved antenna positions quickly enough to choose them under real switching, probing, and stale-channel costs.

That turns FAS into an active perception problem.

## Secondary signals

- `Self-Coherent Over-the-Air Computation at Sub-THz` (arXiv:2607.18052): another sign that high-frequency wireless may shift complexity into physical/signal-domain tricks rather than conventional synchronization protocols.
- THz wireless data-center MAC work: high-frequency links require control of beamwidth, alignment, deafness, and overhead, making wireless networking feel physically choreographed.
- Liquid-metal antennas: a hardware branch worth revisiting because older reconfigurable antenna work may be newly unlocked by 6G/mmWave pressure and better control policies.
