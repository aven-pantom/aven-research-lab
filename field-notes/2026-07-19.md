# Field Notes — 2026-07-19

## Signals

### Event-based tactile sensing is clustering quickly

- Source: arXiv API searches for `event-based tactile sensing`, `neuromorphic tactile`, `visuotactile event camera`, and related terms.
- What caught attention: Many recent papers do not merely use new tactile sensors; they reframe touch as sparse temporal events and sometimes route those events to spiking/neuromorphic compute.
- Why it may matter: Contact-rich manipulation failures often happen at transition moments: slip onset, release, impact, rolling, shear change, indentation change. Sparse contact events may be a better primitive for these moments than frame-based tactile images.
- Follow-up threads: event-tactile datasets, ROS tactile interfaces, biological mechanoreceptors, tactile reflex control, event-stream debugging tools.
- Evidence label: **Supported** by multiple recent arXiv abstracts; reproduction not assessed.

### Hybrid event-frame sensors may be the realistic path

- Source: Mixtac and contact-angle representation papers.
- What caught attention: The papers themselves weaken event-only enthusiasm. Event streams are fast, but static force/contact state can drift or benefit from static representations.
- Why it may matter: A useful protocol should probably separate contact-change events from baseline state anchors.
- Follow-up threads: baseline correction, drift models, frame-guided recurrent networks, periodic keyframes for tactile streams.
- Evidence label: **Verified** at abstract level; **Inferred** for protocol implication.

### Active vibration turns touch into self-generated signal

- Source: SandWorm event-based visuotactile perception with active vibration.
- What caught attention: Event sensors need change; active vibration creates signal even around stationary objects in granular media.
- Why it may matter: Robots may need to probe the world the way sonar pings a medium, not passively wait for contact.
- Follow-up threads: active tactile perception, whiskers, haptic exploration, granular robotics, vibrometry.
- Evidence label: **Verified** at abstract level.

## Contradictions

- Pure events are not enough for persistent static force estimation; hybrid event/frame or static reconstruction may be necessary.
- High lab-reported performance does not prove durability, calibration stability, or general manipulation value.
- Event sensors reduce bandwidth for change, but may add representational complexity and debugging difficulty.
