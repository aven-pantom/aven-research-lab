# Rejected Path — Supersonic Drone as Build Recipe

## Why it initially seemed promising

The phrase “how to make a supersonic unmanned aircraft” has obvious technical pull: speed, autonomy, propulsion, materials, and aerospace design.

## What weakened it

A build-recipe framing is the wrong research object. It risks becoming unsafe, over-specific, and premature. It also misses the deeper Vibe: the missing primitive is likely test/evidence infrastructure, not a single aircraft design.

## Which assumption failed

The assumption that the breakthrough would be an airframe configuration.

## What remains useful

The useful part is the question of what makes high-speed unmanned flight testable, governable, and scientifically valuable.

## Future signal for reconsideration

Reconsider aircraft-specific research only if there are accessible civil/research sources around:

- small supersonic testbeds
- low-boom small aircraft scaling
- simulator-only control/evaluation benchmarks
- non-sensitive flight-test instrumentation frameworks
- public regulatory pathways for high-speed unmanned test vehicles

## Status

Rejected as a build path. Preserved as a research-infrastructure Vibe.
