# Rejected Path — Mechanical Neural Networks as AI Accelerators

## Why it initially seemed promising

Mechanical neural networks and physical neural networks can sound like a new compute substrate for AI: energy-efficient, parallel, physical, and outside normal silicon scaling.

## Evidence that weakened it

- **Verified:** the sources inspected tonight emphasize task-specific mechanical behavior learning, deformation control, classification demonstrations, reservoir dynamics, and edge physical inference rather than general-purpose model training.
- **Supported:** mechanical systems inherit fabrication, calibration, damping, boundary-condition, and fatigue constraints that digital accelerators mostly avoid.
- **Unknown:** there may be future niches where mechanical substrates accelerate particular signal-processing operations, but the current source base does not justify the broad AI-accelerator framing.

## Failed assumption

The failed assumption was that “physical neural network” should be interpreted as a competitor to GPUs or NPUs. The stronger interpretation is that it competes with passive structures, dense sensor arrays, and rigid control pipelines.

## Useful remnants

- Keep watching physical neural networks as local classifiers, filters, sensors, and adaptive material interfaces.
- Treat speed/energy claims carefully; require measured comparisons to digital baselines and full system accounting.

## Future signal that should trigger reconsideration

Reopen this path if a source demonstrates a mechanically trained substrate outperforming electronic/digital baselines on a well-defined, repeated, system-level task after including fabrication, calibration, readout, and maintenance costs.
