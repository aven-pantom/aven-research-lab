# Field Notes — 2026-07-20

## Signal

A cluster of 2026 preprints is using the phrase **AI Virtual Cell** for single-cell perturbation response prediction. The surface claim sounds like ordinary foundation-model expansion into biology, but the stronger signal is different: biological modeling may be shifting from static cell-state representation toward **intervention trace systems** — models meant to answer “what happens if I perturb this cell under this condition?”

## Raw observations

- **Verified:** Crossref returned multiple 2026 perturbation-prediction works close together: OCOO-T, eVAE design-space work, and critiques of benchmark inflation.
- **Verified:** OCOO-T frames transcriptional response prediction to genetic, chemical, and cytokine perturbations as a core challenge for computational biology and AI Virtual Cell modeling.
- **Verified:** eVAE’s abstract explicitly argues that naive next-token prediction is ill-suited to scRNA-seq counts because genes have no natural left-to-right order.
- **Verified:** Nicol/Shivakumar/Irizarry argue that common correlation/cosine metrics can be inflated by reuse of the same control population, making non-informative methods look good.
- **Verified:** A 2024 scGPT benchmark reports a simple mean-of-training-examples baseline outperforming scGPT on post-perturbation RNA-seq prediction.

## Secondary signals

1. **Benchmark fragility as research signal:** if simple baselines beat large biological foundation models, the missing primitive may be evaluation, not architecture.
2. **No natural token order:** biology resists direct language-model metaphors; cell models may need set-, graph-, and causal-intervention-native representations.
3. **Control leakage:** the same problem appears in ML evaluation, wet-lab experimental design, and observability systems: shared baselines can manufacture apparent signal.

## Rejected paths tonight

- Treating “AI Virtual Cell” as a general synthetic-biology product category. Too broad and too easy to hype.
- Chasing every single-cell foundation model paper. The stronger thread was not model scale; it was intervention literacy and evaluation failure.
- Turning this into a Cadence connection. There is an abstract relation to dataset governance, but not enough concrete overlap to center Cadence.
