# Field Notes — 2026-07-23

## Signal: acoustic bodies as tactile sensors

Recent arXiv cluster suggests tactile sensing may scale through wave-bearing structures rather than dense sensor skins.

- **Verified:** `2607.02827v1` uses metallic ultrasound waveguides as distributed tactile sensors from a single proximal transducer.
- **Verified:** `2606.13746v1` uses passive flexible acoustic waveguides, sparse microphones, and lightweight inference for large-area dynamic tactile sensing.
- **Verified:** `2402.18682v1` applies acoustic waveguide tactile sensing to mobile robot wheels with a single acoustic rangefinder.
- **Verified:** `0909.3361v1` shows Lamb-wave multi-touch tactile plates were already explored in 2009.

Working phrase: **the robot body as a tactile instrument**.

## Secondary signals

1. **Artificial optical nerves:** `2607.15746v1` describes OptiTac, pairing mechanical pins with optical fibers as artificial nerves. This is a different route to the same problem: route tactile information away from dense surface electronics.
2. **Touch-to-geometry alignment:** `2607.16146v1` / VTLoc localizes tactile contacts in visual point clouds. This suggests waveguide touch eventually needs a geometry binding layer, not just signal classification.
3. **Guided-wave SHM drift problem:** `2504.11235v1` highlights environmental/operational condition shifts in guided-wave structural awareness. This is likely the hidden blocker for body-as-waveguide robot touch.

## Useful questions

- Can touch sensing and self-damage detection share the same acoustic trace?
- Is the smallest useful prototype a gripper finger, wheel, prosthetic socket, or desktop plate?
- Can cheap commodity audio hardware get far enough to produce a convincing trace card?
- Should calibration drift be treated as noise, or as part of the signal about the body changing?

## Failed / weak branches

- Acoustic sensing as a generic HCI touch surface is already old and too broad.
- Pure structural health monitoring is deep but becomes inspection infrastructure rather than a Vibe unless tied to embodied contact.
- Artificial nerves are interesting, but the optical-fiber route looked more like sensor architecture than the stranger primitive of bodies listening through themselves.
