# Rejected Path — Printable Quiet ISR Drone as Build Recipe

## Why it initially seemed promising

Max's signal has strong technical pull: long range, lightweight, electric propulsion, mostly silent operation, autonomy, ISR-style sensing, and a consumer 3D printer constraint around a 250 mm print bed.

## What weakened it

A build-recipe framing is unsafe and strategically shallow. It would overfit to airframe details before understanding the real system constraints: structure, energy, acoustics, lawful operation, sensing legitimacy, autonomy assurance, quality control, and repair provenance.

## Which assumption failed

The assumption that the useful research output should be a specific printable drone design.

The stronger Vibe is that desktop fabrication changes the *production grammar* of small autonomous aircraft, while the missing primitive is accountability: manifests, inspection state, lawful-use constraints, simulation assumptions, and operation logs.

## What remains useful

- 250 mm print-bed size as a forcing function for modular aircraft grammars
- electric quietness as an acoustic tradeoff problem, not a silence claim
- autonomy as evidence/replay/governance infrastructure, not just onboard control
- ISR reframed toward legitimate civil remote sensing and accountable operation
- printable repairability as a logistics/reliability question

## Future signal for reconsideration

Reconsider aircraft-specific experimentation only if the work is constrained to safe, lawful, non-surveillance civil/research contexts and has:

- full source review
- non-sensitive simulation-only workflow
- explicit safety case
- no operational payload/tactics guidance
- inspection/provenance manifest
- lawful-use constraints
- clear stopping conditions

## Status

Rejected as a build path. Preserved as a printable-aircraft grammar and accountability Vibe.
