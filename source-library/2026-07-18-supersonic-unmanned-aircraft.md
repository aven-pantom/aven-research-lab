# Source Notes — Breakthrough in Drones: How to Make a Supersonic Unmanned Aircraft

## Integrity note

Sources below are real pages retrieved during the session. arXiv/Semantic Scholar searches were rate-limited, so this source map leans on official NASA/company/regulatory-accessible material and labels limits clearly. No experiments are claimed.

## NASA Quesst

- **Title:** Quesst
- **Author or organization:** NASA
- **Publication year:** Living mission page, accessed 2026
- **Link:** https://www.nasa.gov/mission/quesst/
- **Source type:** Official NASA mission page
- **What it contributes:** Establishes NASA’s low-boom supersonic flight effort and frames the mission around data that could make supersonic flight over land possible.
- **Reliability notes:** Official source; mission framing is reliable, but it is not about unmanned drones specifically.
- **Relevant finding:** NASA says Quesst is collecting data that could make supersonic flight over land possible by addressing the sonic-boom/noise problem.

## NASA Supersonic Flight

- **Title:** Supersonic Flight
- **Author or organization:** NASA
- **Publication year:** Living NASA topic page, accessed 2026
- **Link:** https://www.nasa.gov/aeronautics/supersonic-flight/
- **Source type:** Official NASA topic page
- **What it contributes:** Connects commercial supersonic flight over land with Quesst and X-59.
- **Reliability notes:** Official source; broad topic overview, not an engineering spec.
- **Relevant finding:** NASA describes work with partners to enable new choices for high-speed air travel, starting with commercial supersonic flight over land through Quesst and X-59.

## Boom XB-1

- **Publication year:** Living company page, accessed 2026
- **Link:** https://boomsupersonic.com/xb-1
